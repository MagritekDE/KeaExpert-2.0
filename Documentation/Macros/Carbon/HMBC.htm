HMBC

 

The HMBC (Heteronuclear Multiple Bond Correlation) experiment establishes correlations between carbons and protons that are separated by two or three bonds, and occasionally four bonds in conjugated systems. Direct one-bond correlations are suppressed, focusing instead on longer-range couplings.

HMBC is widely used in structural elucidation of organic molecules, especially for:

  • Determining connectivity between protons and carbons separated by multiple bonds.
  • Distinguishing between different molecular fragments based on long-range correlations.
  • Providing complementary information to HSQC, which shows direct (one-bond) correlations.
  • Detecting quaternary carbons that lack directly attached protons.

 

Pulse sequence

Here:

  • τ should be set to match one-bond 1H-13C coupling constant, 0.5/J1 (~ 140 Hz)
  • Δ is set to match an average of all long-range 1H-13C coupling constant (~8 Hz).
  • t-time evolution encodes the heteronucleus frequency along f1 axis.
  • Phase cycling or gradients are used for artefact suppression.

The HMBC sequence comprises several blocks. The first block incorporates a low-pass J-coupling filter, which consists of a delay τ and 90-degree 13C pulse that is applied after the initial 90-degree 1H pulse. This filter suppresses one-bond coupling 1JCH , which may interfere with long-range coherences under interest. Usually, this delay is set to the value of about 3.5 ms, corresponding to a coupling constant of 140 Hz. The next building block of the HMBC is long-range evolution interval Δ. This evolution delay should be optimized to Δ~1/nJCH.

The next step of the HMBC sequence is t1-evolution period for transferring 13C chemical shift information. The last block of HMBC, which consist a 90-degree pulse applied on 13C channel, converts the encoded magnetization into observable single-quantum magnetization. Heteronuclear decoupling may be applied during detection to simplify spectra.

Tips and Tricks

  • Optimize J-coupling delay carefully (too short: weak cross-peaks; too long: loss of magnetization).
  • Use selective long-range J-filters to suppress unwanted one-bond correlations.

 

Parameters

Note that some parameters may be hidden (see View menu option Toggle parameter visibility)

Some HMBC specific parameters:

Pulse length for all (us) pulse duration for both 1H and 13C channels

J(C,H) (Hz) one-bond 1H-13C coupling constant

^(n)J(C,H) (Hz) - an average of all long-range 1H-13C coupling constant

Center freq. f1 (ppm) an offset added to the 13C frequency

Center freq. f2 (ppm) an offset added to the 1H frequency
